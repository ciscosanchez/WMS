# Armstrong WMS Opportunity Overview

## Purpose

This presentation is designed for Armstrong leadership as an overview of what could be possible with a modern warehouse platform. It is intentionally future-facing, operational, and light on implementation detail.

---

## Slide 1: Title

**Armstrong WMS**
From Receiving Through Fulfillment, Billing, and Client Visibility

**Presenter subtitle**
What a modern Armstrong warehouse platform could make possible

**Speaker notes**
This is not just a warehouse system pitch. It is a view of how Armstrong could connect receiving, inventory, fulfillment, transportation coordination, customer visibility, and billing into one operating model.

---

## Slide 2: The Opportunity

**Headline**
Armstrong has an opportunity to unify freight-oriented warehouse operations and modern fulfillment in one platform.

**On-slide points**
- Support both freight/3PL receiving and DTC or B2B fulfillment in the same environment
- Replace fragmented handoffs between spreadsheets, paper, point solutions, and manual re-entry
- Give operators, managers, customers, and finance a shared source of operational truth
- Create a foundation for faster onboarding, better reporting, and cleaner billing

**Speaker notes**
Most systems are strong in only one direction. Freight-heavy tools tend to feel legacy and operationally rigid. E-commerce tools tend to ignore freight complexity. Armstrong’s opportunity is to bridge both.

---

## Slide 3: Vision For The Armstrong Ecosystem

**Headline**
The WMS becomes the warehouse execution layer inside a broader Armstrong digital ecosystem.

**On-slide points**
- NetSuite remains system of record for financials, billing, AR/AP, and contracts
- DispatchPro remains the transportation execution system
- Armstrong WMS manages receiving, inventory, warehouse execution, and fulfillment
- Operator mobile workflows support floor execution
- Client portal exposes inventory, orders, shipments, and billing visibility
- Integration hub connects marketplaces, EDI, carriers, and internal systems

**Speaker notes**
The goal is not to replace every system. The goal is to give each system a clear role and remove re-keying between them.

---

## Slide 4: What The Platform Could Cover

**Headline**
One platform can support the full warehouse lifecycle from inbound identification to outbound cash events.

**On-slide points**
- Customer onboarding, rate-card setup, and client-specific operating rules
- ASN creation, dock arrival, unloading, discrepancy capture, and putaway
- Real-time inventory by client, warehouse, bin, lot, and serial
- Order intake from portal, API, marketplace, or EDI
- Picking, packing, shipping, and tracking confirmation
- Billable warehouse events exported into NetSuite for invoicing

**Speaker notes**
This is where Armstrong starts to move from operational visibility to operational control. Every warehouse action can become a tracked event, and every tracked event can become a reportable and billable outcome.

---

## Slide 5: Inbound Advantage

**Headline**
Armstrong can be unusually strong on receiving because the design starts with freight reality, not just e-commerce assumptions.

**On-slide points**
- ASN-driven receiving workflow from expected to arrived to receiving to completed
- Support for BOLs, PO references, inspections, discrepancy logging, and staged putaway
- Capture shortages, overages, damage, quarantine, lot, and serial detail
- Create an auditable transaction trail from dock activity into inventory

**Speaker notes**
This matters because receiving is where warehouse accuracy begins. If inbound is weak, everything downstream gets slower and less reliable.

---

## Slide 6: AI-Powered Document Intake

**Headline**
Document intelligence could remove manual keying from receiving.

**On-slide points**
- Capture BOLs, packing lists, invoices, and other inbound documents from phone, scanner, email, or upload
- Extract structured fields with confidence scoring
- Present side-by-side human review for exceptions
- Post verified data into ASN and receiving workflows
- Reduce dock-side delays caused by manual document entry

**Speaker notes**
This is one of the clearest early automation wins. Instead of spending 5 to 15 minutes keying each document, teams review exceptions and move freight faster.

---

## Slide 7: Fulfillment And Outbound Execution

**Headline**
The same environment can support modern fulfillment operations without separating the freight side from the order side.

**On-slide points**
- Intake orders from client portal, Shopify, Amazon, Walmart, API, or EDI
- Allocate inventory and generate single-order, batch, wave, or zone picks
- Execute pick, pack, and ship workflows on mobile or warehouse devices
- Rate shop carriers, generate labels, and return tracking
- Support parcel, LTL/FTL, and white-glove outbound scenarios

**Speaker notes**
For Armstrong, this means one warehouse platform can support traditional 3PL work and growth into higher-velocity fulfillment models.

---

## Slide 8: Client And Operator Experience

**Headline**
Different users can work in the same platform through role-specific experiences.

**On-slide points**
- Warehouse managers use the back-office WMS
- Floor workers use a mobile-optimized operator experience for receive, pick, pack, move, and count
- Customers use a portal for inventory, orders, shipments, reports, and billing visibility
- Armstrong platform admins manage tenants, access, and cross-client controls

**Speaker notes**
This avoids separate products with separate logic. One codebase, one data model, different views for different users.

---

## Slide 9: Why Armstrong Could Differentiate

**Headline**
The market gap is the combination of freight depth, fulfillment breadth, and modern usability.

**On-slide points**
- Logiwa is strong in e-commerce but not built around freight-heavy receiving
- Magaya is freight-oriented but widely seen as legacy and implementation-heavy
- NetSuite WMS is powerful for ERP-centered companies but not purpose-built for multi-client 3PL execution
- Extensiv is 3PL-focused but weaker on freight depth and modern flexibility
- Armstrong can occupy the space between freight sophistication and modern fulfillment

**Speaker notes**
This is the core strategic position: not trying to out-feature every incumbent in their strongest niche, but solving the combination they do not solve well together.

---

## Slide 10: Architecture That Fits The Business

**Headline**
The platform architecture is designed for multi-client operations, operational control, and practical deployment.

**On-slide points**
- Schema-per-tenant architecture for strong client data isolation
- Shared core for inventory, warehouse, products, clients, audit, and sequences
- Support for both freight workflows and fulfillment workflows in the same tenant
- Containerized deployment model from local development to AWS production
- Ability to self-host and control total cost of ownership

**Speaker notes**
The important point for leadership is not the technology brand names. It is that the platform is structured for secure multi-client operations and can be deployed in a straightforward, repeatable way.

---

## Slide 11: What This Could Unlock For Armstrong

**Headline**
The business value is not just software replacement. It is a better operating model.

**On-slide points**
- Faster receiving with fewer manual bottlenecks
- Better inventory accuracy and traceability
- More scalable fulfillment without adding disconnected tools
- Stronger customer experience through self-service visibility
- Cleaner handoff of warehouse billable events into finance
- Better readiness for growth across facilities, customers, and service lines

**Speaker notes**
This is the shift from reactive warehouse management to a platform that supports scale, margin control, and a more modern customer experience.

---

## Slide 12: Phased Roadmap

**Headline**
A practical rollout can sequence value instead of trying to do everything at once.

**On-slide points**
- Phase 1: Core WMS foundation, receiving, inventory, and warehouse controls
- Phase 2: Operator mobile workflows and discrepancy-driven receiving execution
- Phase 3: Fulfillment, carrier integrations, and outbound tracking
- Phase 4: Client portal, billing-event export, and NetSuite operational integration
- Phase 5: Document intelligence, marketplace connectors, and deeper automation

**Speaker notes**
This gives Armstrong an incremental path. Start with warehouse control, then add user experience, integrations, and automation in a sequence that compounds value.

---

## Slide 13: Closing

**Headline**
Armstrong can build a modern warehouse platform around how its business actually operates.

**On-slide points**
- One platform for receiving, inventory, fulfillment, and customer visibility
- Built around Armstrong’s operating model, not generic software assumptions
- Positioned to connect warehouse execution with transportation and financial systems
- Creates a foundation for long-term operational and commercial differentiation

**Speaker notes**
The message to leave with is simple: this is a realistic path to a more integrated Armstrong operating platform, not just a software implementation.
